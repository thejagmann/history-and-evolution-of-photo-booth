How to Trace the Fascinating History and Evolution of Photo Booths

How to Trace the Fascinating History and Evolution of Photo Booths

Posted by on 2023-11-24

The history of photo booths is an intriguing one, full of evolution and innovation. Starting with the first ever "Photomaton" in 1925, these automated machines have come a long way to become the modern-day phenomenon they are today.

The Photomaton was invented by Anatol Josepho, a Russian immigrant living in New York City. This early version of the photo booth allowed customers to insert coins and take eight passport-style photos in a matter of minutes. Innovations such as color film, improved lighting, 1-minute exposure times and improved lenses made this machine incredibly popular throughout Europe and America during the 1930s.

In 1947, three men from Kansas City unveiled the first "modern" type of booth — featuring two seated positions instead of one standing position — which became known as the "Glamour Booth." This new design allowed couples or groups to be photographed together for keepsake images that could be shared with friends and family.

By 1965, over 20,000 booths were in use across America alone. The 1970s saw digital technology enter into play: By 1975 computerized models had been released that could store up to 30 pictures on magnetic cards. In 1989 Sony introduced its own line of automated kiosks called “Sony Image Station” which featured video image capture capabilities along with traditional still photography features.

Today we find photo booths at all sorts of events – birthdays, weddings, graduations – but also in places like shopping malls or arcades for people to take fun selfies or group shots with friends on special occasions. With Instagram filters now available on many modern machines we can expect them to remain popular for years to come!
